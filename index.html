Entry No. 1  —  Early Childhood (6 years old and below)

Six years in a
millisecond.

the early years — blurry, warm, whole.

I don't really remember most of it.
I just remember it felt okay. More than okay.

Honestly, the first six years of my life are mostly a blur. I know the facts more than I actually remember them. I lived with my grandparents, my mother was away, I spent most of my time outside. But when I try to actually picture it, the memories are hazy. Like trying to remember a dream after you've been awake for hours or like a lowered resolution of my life. The feelings are there. The details, not so much.

but the feeling, that part I still remember

What I do remember is this: I called my grandparents Mama and Papa, and it never felt strange. They weren't my grandparents in any way that mattered to me back then they were just my parents. That's all I knew. Mama was the one who was there, and Papa was the one who was there too. That was my whole world, and it was enough. It was more than enough.

Mama.
My grandmother. But just Mama to me. The one who fed me, the one whose house smelled like home. Still does.
I didn't know to miss anything, she was everything
Papa.
My grandfather. But just Papa to me. I was always excited when he comes home from work at 5pm.
never had to wonder if he'd be there, he just was
&

Outside was everything. I don't know how to explain what that was like to someone who grew up differently, the mud, the grass, playing in the rain, and the smell of the earth after it rained. We played marbles on the ground, collected stickers like they were treasure, had spider fights (I wish i was still brave like how i was back then, i have arachnophobia now). It sounds so small now but back then it was everything. That was the whole world. That patch of ground with the neighbor's kids was all I needed.

...continued marbles, stickers, spiders, that was life

There were no problems yet. I think that's the thing I miss most when I look back at that time, not the games, not even the people, but just the feeling of having no problems. Nothing was complicated. Nothing hurt in that quiet, lingering way things hurt when you're older. I was just a kid playing outside, and Mama and Papa were just inside waiting, and that was the whole of it.

I do remember one clear moment, sitting at a desk somewhere,
learning to write my first letters. My mother was there.
I don't remember the school. I barely remember what i wrote, I think it was those single paper of a lower and an uppercase of a letter.
But I remember she was watching, and I remember wanting
to get it right because she was.

That might be the first time I felt like someone was proud of me.

When I think about who I was at that age, I think I was just, unbothered. Happy in that simple, unthinking way kids are happy when they don't know anything is missing. And I think that came from Mama and Papa. They gave me a home that felt so complete that I didn't go looking for what wasn't there. That's not a small thing. I didn't realize I'm only starting to realize how big that actually is.

Six years felt like a millisecond
probably because none of it felt like something I had to survive.
It was just life. Easy, muddy, warm life.

I miss that version of me sometimes.
the one who didn't know problems existed yet.
